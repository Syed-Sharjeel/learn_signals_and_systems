## Objective

**Time Domain Analysis of Second-Order Circuit**

To simulate, understand, and validate the zero-input response of an RLC series circuit.

## Theory

### Second-Order Circuits

Second-order circuits are electrical circuits that contain a resistor (R), inductor (L), and capacitor (C), connected either in series or in parallel.

These circuits are described by second-order differential equations due to the presence of two energy storage elements (inductor and capacitor).

### Time Constant

The time constant represents how quickly a circuit responds to changes over time.

* For an RC circuit: $$\tau = RC$$

* For an RL circuit: $$\tau = \frac{L}{R}$$

Where:

* $${R}$$ = resistance
* $${L}$$ = inductance
* $${C}$$ = capacitance
